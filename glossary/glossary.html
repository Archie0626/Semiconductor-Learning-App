Glossary

Buzzwords Decoded – From PN to OMG!

  • Analog Circuit

    A circuit that processes continuous signals.

  • Anode

    Positive terminal of a diode.

  • ASIC (Application-Specific Integrated Circuit)

    A custom chip designed for a specific application.

  • Band Gap

    Energy difference between valence and conduction bands; determines conductivity.

  • Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT)

    A transistor with three layers used for amplification.

  • Bulk Semiconductor

    A semiconductor in its original, undoped state.

  • CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor)

    A low-power logic technology used in most ICs.

  • Carrier Mobility

    How quickly electrons/holes can move through a material.

  • Crystal Lattice

    The organized structure of atoms in a semiconductor.

  • Diode

    A two-terminal component allowing current in one direction only.

  • Doping

    The process of adding impurities to a semiconductor to change its conductivity.

  • Die

    A small block of semiconductor material on which a circuit is fabricated.

  • EEPROM (Electrically Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory)

    Non-volatile memory that can be rewritten electronically.

  • Electrons

    Negatively charged particles responsible for current in semiconductors.

  • Etching

    A fabrication process to remove layers from a wafer surface.

  • Fab (Fabrication Plant)

    A factory where semiconductor wafers are manufactured.

  • FET (Field Effect Transistor)

    A type of transistor controlled by electric field.

  • Flip-Flop

    A basic memory element in digital electronics.

  • Gate

    The control terminal in transistors like MOSFETs.

  • Gallium Arsenide (GaAs)

    A compound semiconductor with high-speed properties.

  • Ground

    A reference point in electrical circuits.

  • Hall Effect

    A phenomenon used to measure carrier concentration and magnetic fields.

  • Heterojunction

    A junction between two different semiconductor materials.

  • Hybrid IC

    A circuit with both discrete components and integrated circuits.

  • IC (Integrated Circuit)

    A complete circuit built on a single chip.

  • Insulator

    Material that resists electrical current flow.

  • Intrinsic Semiconductor

    A pure semiconductor without doping.

  • Junction

    Interface between two semiconductor regions (e.g., PN junction).

  • JFET (Junction Field Effect Transistor)

    A FET with a PN junction.

  • Junction Capacitance

    Capacitance due to depletion region in a PN junction.

  • Kirk Effect

    A phenomenon in BJTs where base widening affects performance at high currents.

  • Karnaugh Map

    A method to simplify Boolean algebra expressions (logic design).

  • LED (Light Emitting Diode)

    A diode that emits light when forward biased.

  • Lithography

    A process to transfer circuit patterns onto semiconductor wafers.

  • Load Line

    A graphical method to analyze transistor circuits.

  • MOSFET (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor FET)

    A widely used type of transistor.

  • Moore's Law

    The prediction that the number of transistors on a chip doubles every 2 years.

  • Monolithic IC

    An IC where all components are on a single chip.

  • n-Type Semiconductor

    Semiconductor doped with donors (extra electrons).

  • Noise Margin

    The tolerance level for noise in logic circuits.

  • Node

    A point in a circuit where two or more components are connected.

  • Ohmic Contact

    A contact that allows current to flow equally in both directions.

  • Op-Amp (Operational Amplifier)

    A high-gain voltage amplifier.

  • Oxidation

    A process to grow an oxide layer (e.g., silicon dioxide) on a wafer.

  • p-Type Semiconductor

    Semiconductor doped with acceptors (holes).

  • Photolithography

    Light-based process to define patterns on wafers.

  • PN Junction

    The boundary between p-type and n-type materials.

  • Quantum Tunneling

    A quantum phenomenon where particles pass through barriers.

  • Quiescent Point (Q-Point)

    The DC operating point of a transistor.

  • Quantum Dot

    A nanoscale semiconductor particle with quantum properties.

  • Resistor

    A component that resists current flow.

  • Rectifier

    A device (like a diode) that converts AC to DC.

  • RC Delay

    The time delay caused by resistance and capacitance in a circuit.

  • Semiconductor

    A material with conductivity between conductors and insulators.

  • SCR (Silicon Controlled Rectifier)

    A four-layer diode-like component used in power control.

  • Substrate

    The base layer on which circuits are built.

  • Transistor

    A semiconductor device used for switching and amplification.

  • Threshold Voltage (Vth)

    The voltage needed to turn on a MOSFET.

  • Thin Film

    Layers of material a few nanometers thick used in chip fabrication.

  • ULSI (Ultra-Large-Scale Integration)

    Integration of millions of transistors on a chip.

  • UV Lithography

    Using ultraviolet light in photolithography processes.

  • Unijunction Transistor (UJT)

    A special transistor used in timing circuits.

  • Valence Band

    The highest range of electron energies in which electrons are normally present.

  • VLSI (Very-Large-Scale Integration)

    Technology that integrates thousands of components on a chip.

  • Voltage Regulator

    A circuit that maintains constant voltage.

  • Wafer

    A thin slice of semiconductor material used for chip fabrication.

  • Wafer Dicing

    Cutting wafers into individual dies.

  • White Noise

    Random noise with equal intensity across frequencies.

  • X-FAB

    A semiconductor foundry company.

  • X-Ray Lithography

    A photolithography technique using X-rays for higher resolution.

  • Yield

    The percentage of working chips on a wafer.

  • Yttrium

    Sometimes used in specialized semiconductor materials and devices.

  • Zener Diode

    A diode that allows current in reverse when a specific voltage is reached.

  • Zero-Bias

    A condition where no external voltage is applied.

  • Zigbee

    A wireless protocol that uses semiconductors for IoT applications.
